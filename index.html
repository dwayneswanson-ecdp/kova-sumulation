What Is This Activity?

In this activity, you will practise leadership and management skills in a realistic business scenario. You will listen to a series of short voice messages from different people at a company called KOVA. Each message gives you part of the story. Together, they describe a crisis that the company must manage quickly.

After listening, you will work in a small group. Each group takes the perspective of a different team. You will discuss the situation from your group's point of view and prepare a presentation to share with the class.

⚠️ This is a critical and urgent issue inside the company. Every person in the organisation is affected — and a decision must be made in the next few hours.

2
How the Activity Works
  1. 1.
    Listen to all five voice messages in order. You may listen twice. Take notes — details are important.
  2. 2.
    Find your group. Your teacher will assign you a group number. Each group represents a different team or perspective.
  3. 3.
    Read your group brief using the dropdown in Section 4. It tells you who you are, which messages to focus on, and what to discuss.
  4. 4.
    Discuss within your group and agree on your position and recommendations for each question in your brief.
  5. 5.
    Present your conclusions to the class. Be ready to explain your decisions and respond to questions from other groups.

Group 1
Executive Leadership Team

Decision Makers

Who Are You?

You are the senior leadership team at KOVA. You are responsible for the business, the product, the team, and the company's reputation. You must walk into the noon meeting and make a decision. The press event is in 48 hours, the investor is flying in, and the client is waiting for your call.

🎧 Focus on These Messages

MSG 2 Marcus — what is technically possible?
MSG 3 Dr. Jana — what does the CEO want?
MSG 4 Thomas Richter — what does the client expect?
MSG 5 Sara — what is the state of the team?

Questions to Answer in Your Presentation

  • 1.
    Launch Decision Do you launch in 48 hours, delay, or find another solution? Give clear reasons using what you heard.
  • 2.
    Client Communication What do you say to Thomas Richter at Charité Berlin, and when? Who makes that call?
  • 3.
    Investor Management A Series B investor is flying in for the press event. How do you handle this?
  • 4.
    Priorities & Trade-offs What are you choosing between — safety, reputation, money, or the team? Be honest about what you are giving up.
  • 5.
    Next Steps What are the three most important actions your team must take in the next 12 hours?
Group 2
Line Management Team
You manage the engineers directly. You are the bridge between the developers and the boardroom.
People Managers

Who Are You?

You are the direct managers of the engineering team, led by Marcus Klein. You know the technical details better than the executives. You also know that your team is exhausted — and that Lena, and others, are close to quitting. You must protect your people and give leadership a realistic picture of what is possible.

🎧 Focus on These Messages

MSG 1 Lena — what exactly is the bug?
MSG 2 Marcus — what is the honest timeline?
MSG 5 Sara — what does HR know about the team?

Questions to Answer in Your Presentation

  • 1.
    Technical Reality Based on what Lena and Marcus said, what can realistically be fixed in 48 hours — and what cannot?
  • 2.
    Protecting the Team What immediate actions do you take to prevent more resignations? What do you say to Lena today?
  • 3.
    Was Escalation Handled Well? Marcus felt pressure to launch with a disclaimer. Was that the right response? What would you have done differently?
  • 4.
    Accountability At what point did management fail the team — and how do you address this without destroying trust?
  • 5.
    Recovery Plan If the launch is delayed 3 weeks, how do you organise the work without burning out the remaining team?
Group 3

Culture & Wellbeing

Who Are You?

You are the HR department at KOVA, led by Sara Osei. You have one formal resignation letter on your desk. Two other employees are considering leaving. You know that the team raised concerns in February — and nobody acted. You have a seat at the noon meeting and a responsibility to speak up.

🎧 Focus on These Messages

MSG 1 Lena — how does she feel? what does she need?
MSG 5 Sara — what has HR observed?

Questions to Answer in Your Presentation

  • 1.
    Immediate Response What do you do in the next 24 hours to stabilise morale? Who do you speak to first?
  • 2.
    The Resignation How do you respond to the formal resignation letter? Do you try to keep this person — and if so, how?
  • 3.
    Culture Diagnosis Concerns were raised in February and ignored. What does this tell you about how KOVA manages feedback and accountability?
  • 4.
    Leadership Message What do you recommend Dr. Jana Kovač communicate to the whole company today — and how?
  • 5.
    Long-term Change What one process or policy would prevent this situation from happening again?
